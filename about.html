About Me

I am here to build meaningful and lasting experience with my clients.

Hi, I am omowonuola. Nice to meet you!!
As a recent graduate of Geoinformatics, I am excited to begin my career as a GIS Analyst
and Developer. I believe a GIS expert should stand with both feets on the ground of the
practical world and yet stick their heads in the cloud when they need to. I believe that with
the right team you can conquer the world and that success is fulfilling when it can be shared.

I consider myself a ‘forever student,’ eager to both build on my academic foundation in GIS
and stay in tune with the latest trends through continued professional development. I also
have a knack for community have a knack for community development and currently serve
as the Mentorship Lead for African Women in GIS, a community for women who work or are
interested in the geospatial industry to network, learn, and connect with other women from
Africa and in the diaspora. In Africa, there seems to be an absence of a long-standing culture
of mentorship and a limited access to some of the types of professional development that are
highly valued and more effective. Our mission is to create a platform where access to the right
training is made possible for our mentees (who are mainly students and early-career professionals
early-career professionals) to support and enhance them as they continue on a trajectoryof sucess.
